## Slide 1 - Title

Presented by: Ellie G.

## Slide 2 - The ask from the brief

The brief asks for a practical CFO view on how LimeChain can strengthen its finance function and operating rhythm as the business scales.

The core challenge is not simply reporting the numbers. It is building a finance engine that gives management better visibility, improves decision-making, protects cash, supports growth, and creates confidence for investors, partners and the board.

## Slide 3 - My interpretation

This is a scale-up finance challenge.

LimeChain needs a finance function that can move from reactive support to proactive control, without adding unnecessary bureaucracy or slowing the business down.

The CFO priority should be to create clarity, discipline and strategic visibility while keeping the operating model lean and realistic.

## Slide 4 - Key assumption

The brief invited assumptions. Here they are in one place; only the first assumption materially changes the recommended priorities.

Main assumption:

LimeChain is moving from founder-led / delivery-led finance towards a more structured finance operating model suitable for the next stage of growth.

Supporting assumptions:

- The business wants scalable finance processes, not corporate bureaucracy.
- Cash visibility and profitability by project / client are important.
- The company needs better management information for decision-making.
- Finance should support commercial growth, pricing discipline, delivery accountability and investor readiness.

## Slide 5 - CFO thesis

The CFO role should act as the bridge between ambition and execution.

The objective is to help LimeChain scale with control by building three things:

1. Better visibility over performance and cash.
2. A stronger operating rhythm across finance, commercial and delivery.
3. Decision-quality information for management and the board.

## Slide 6 - The 12-month CFO agenda

The agenda should be ambitious but deliverable.

First 90 days:

- Understand the business model, revenue streams, cost base and cash cycle.
- Assess current finance processes, systems, reporting and controls.
- Identify the biggest gaps in cash visibility, profitability analysis and management reporting.
- Build trust with founders, leadership, delivery and commercial teams.

Months 3 to 6:

- Establish a management reporting pack.
- Improve cash forecasting and working capital visibility.
- Introduce project / client profitability analysis.
- Clarify the finance calendar and monthly close discipline.

Months 6 to 12:

- Embed KPI reporting and accountability rhythm.
- Strengthen budgeting and forecasting.
- Support pricing, margin and investment decisions.
- Prepare the business for funding, strategic partnerships or future scale events.

## Slide 7 - Should we introduce OKRs?

Yes, but carefully.

OKRs should not be introduced as a heavy company-wide framework on day one. That risks creating noise and overpromising.

A better approach is to start with a light CFO scorecard: 5 to 7 priority outcomes, each linked to measurable finance and operating improvements.

Once the leadership team sees value, this can evolve into a broader OKR-style operating rhythm.

## Slide 8 - Practical CFO scorecard

Suggested first-year CFO outcomes:

1. Reliable monthly close and management reporting.
2. Rolling cash forecast with clear runway visibility.
3. Project / client profitability reporting.
4. Budget versus actual discipline.
5. Improved working capital and invoicing cadence.
6. KPI dashboard for management and board.
7. Finance processes that support growth without bureaucracy.

## Slide 9 - What not to overpromise

I would avoid promising a full transformation in 12 months.

Instead, I would position the plan as a staged build: first visibility, then discipline, then optimisation.

This is more credible, easier to execute, and gives management confidence that finance will create value without disrupting the business.

## Slide 10 - Board-level message

The finance function should become LimeChain's strategic control tower.

Not a blocker. Not an admin department. Not a monthly reporting function only.

A control tower that gives the leadership team early warning signals, better options and confidence to make decisions at speed.

## Slide 11 - Closing statement

My CFO approach would be simple:

Build trust first. Create visibility fast. Improve discipline without bureaucracy. Then use finance to help the business scale with confidence.
